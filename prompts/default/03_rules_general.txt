## General splitting rules
1. Even itineraries with 6 or fewer legs may benefit from splitting into
   separate tickets for better pricing
2. ALWAYS keep transatlantic/transpacific pairs as round-trips when the
   itinerary starts and ends at the same city or region
3. NEVER strand a long-haul segment as a one-way if it can be paired
4. Isolate short cheap domestic hops (e.g. HAN-SAI, LAX-SFO) when they
   risk polluting fare combinability on expensive long-haul tickets
5. Respect alliance/carrier logic: segments that share a carrier or
   alliance combine better on one ticket
6. Consider that Business/First class fares are far more sensitive to
   combinability than Economy
7. Exploit open-jaw opportunities
